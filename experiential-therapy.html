Experiential Therapy

Activity-based and outdoor interventions for meaningful, lasting change

What Is Experiential Therapy?

Experiential therapy is a therapeutic approach that uses activity-based and outdoor modalities to help clients practice key life skills — not just in conversation, but in real time. This approach can make the therapeutic process more approachable and effective, particularly for those who may not respond as well to traditional office-based talk therapy.

I integrate experiential therapy into my practice as a way to meet clients where they are — using the activities and environments they find meaningful and engaging as vehicles for therapeutic growth and healing.

Examples of Experiential Therapy Activities

🐕

In-Vivo Exposure Therapy

Real-world exposure exercises for anxiety, phobias, and obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD). For example, accompanying a client with a fear of dogs on a walk — practicing coping skills in the moment, not just in the office.

🥾

Outdoor Activities

Hiking, mountain biking, rock climbing, and disc golf serve as contexts for therapeutic conversation and behavioral activation — helping clients become more engaged with life, their emotions, and their strengths.

🎵

Creative Exercises

Activities like songwriting can be used for trauma-focused cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT), facilitating narrative reprocessing in a way that feels safe, expressive, and meaningful to the client.

♟️

Hands-On Office Activities

Structured activities like chess become therapeutic tools — providing a space to challenge depressive thoughts, develop problem-solving skills, and build confidence through mastery and engagement.

When Might Experiential Therapy Be a Good Fit?

Experiential therapy may be especially useful in the following situations:

  • ✦
    For clients who have struggled with traditional talk therapy and would benefit from a more active, engaging approach.
  • ✦
    When supported practice of behavioral activation is needed — helping clients re-engage with meaningful activities and build momentum.
  • ✦
    For clients who benefit from real-world, practical application of key skills — practicing new behaviors in context, rather than only discussing them conceptually.
  • ✦
    When outdoor settings or physical activities help clients feel more comfortable, open, and engaged in the therapeutic process.

Personalized to Each Client

My experiential therapy approach is personalized to the individual, integrating each client's interests and preferences to maximize engagement and outcomes. Whether a young person loves hiking, creative expression, sports, or strategy games, I find ways to weave those interests into the therapeutic experience.

This approach combines activity-based therapy with warmth, compassion, and a strengths-based perspective — ensuring that each session is both clinically effective and personally meaningful.

Evidence-Based Foundation

All experiential therapy activities are grounded in evidence-based clinical methods. The activities serve as vehicles for proven therapeutic techniques — including cognitive-behavioral therapy, exposure therapy, behavioral activation, and trauma-focused interventions.
