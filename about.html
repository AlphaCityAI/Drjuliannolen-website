About Me

Dedicated to the well-being of children, adolescents, and families

Julian Nolen, Ph.D.

I am a licensed psychologist and the owner of Montana Counseling and Assessment, PLLC, located in Helena, Montana. With over 20 years of professional experience, I specialize in clinical and developmental psychology, focusing on the development and implementation of effective mental health interventions at the individual, family, and community levels.

My therapeutic approach integrates evidence-based and experiential interventions with compassion, warmth, and a strengths-based perspective. I am committed to providing effective psychological services with humor and a dedication to real-life application for the well-being of children, adolescents, young adults, and families.

I have supported young people and families across a wide range of settings — including maximum security youth corrections, chronic illness clinics, public high schools, shelters, and private practice — bringing depth, versatility, and understanding to my clinical work.

Education

I hold two M.S. degrees and completed my Ph.D. at Colorado State University in 2015. My doctoral work focused on clinical and developmental psychology with an emphasis on children's and adolescent mental health.

Clinical Interests

Development and implementation of effective mental health interventions for children, adolescents, and families — combining evidence-based methods with engaging, practical, and enjoyable therapeutic approaches.

Academic & Teaching Role

In addition to my clinical practice, I serve as the Department Chair and Internship Coordinator for the Psychology Department at Carroll College, where I hold the title of Associate Professor. I am passionate about training the next generation of mental health professionals and bridging clinical practice with education.

Therapeutic Approach

I combine evidence-based therapeutic methods with engaging, practical, and enjoyable interventions. Whether working in my office or in outdoor, experiential settings, I bring warmth, humor, and a commitment to real-life application to every session.

My strengths-based perspective emphasizes building on each client's existing abilities and resources, fostering independence and confidence. My ultimate goal is to help every client and family develop the tools they need to thrive — and to graduate from therapy equipped with lasting resilience.
