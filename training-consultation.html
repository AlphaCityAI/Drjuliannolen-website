Training & Consultation

Educational services related to child and adolescent health and development

Overview

In addition to my clinical work, I offer education and training services for organizations seeking to improve knowledge and best practices regarding issues related to child and adolescent health and development. My training services draw from both established best practices and my own research and clinical experiences working with children, adolescents, and families over the past 17+ years.

My training style is interactive, engaging, and designed to make complex topics approachable and meaningful. I incorporate multisensory presentations, audience participation, enthusiasm, and humor, with the goal of equipping organizations and their staff not just with information, but with practical, usable skills.

Organizations can request tailored education and training services based on their specific interests and needs. Fees are determined by factors such as time commitment and travel requirements.

Available Trainings

Select a topic to view a description.

Trauma-Informed Care in Communities and Schools

This training supports organizations and care providers in understanding the emotional, neuropsychological, and behavioral impacts of psychological trauma. It emphasizes compassionate and effective intervention both at the individual and community level. The training duration varies from 2 to 8 hours, depending on organizational need.

Strengths of the Adolescent Brain: Supporting Psychological Well-Being Among Teenage Populations

Unlike most content that focuses on challenges and risks during adolescent brain development, this seminar highlights the strengths of teenagers. It is designed to help parents, educators, and mental health providers harness and encourage these strengths to promote well-being and healthy maturation. This training lasts about 2 hours.

Through the Looking Glass: Compassion and Case Conceptualization in Working with Difficult Youth

This is an in-depth training using real case examples to foster compassionate and effective case conceptualization, treatment planning, and intervention for challenging youth. It typically runs 2–4 hours, tailored to the needs of the organization.

Resilience in Trauma-Exposed Youth

Building interventions that foster resilience by leveraging existing strengths and protective factors in children and families.

Self-Care for Care Providers

Emphasizing the importance of self-care for professionals who work with high-need populations.
