Clinical Services

Evidence-based care for children, adolescents, young adults, and families

Overview

Dr. Nolen offers a range of psychological services designed to meet the unique needs of children, adolescents, young adults, and families. His approach integrates evidence-based therapeutic techniques with compassion, warmth, and a strengths-based perspective. Each service is tailored to the individual, with the ultimate goal of helping clients develop the skills and confidence to manage life's challenges independently.

💬

Individual Psychotherapy

One-on-one therapy sessions for children, adolescents, and young adults dealing with emotional, behavioral, developmental, and learning challenges. Sessions integrate evidence-based methods with warmth and practical application to build lasting skills and resilience.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Family Therapy

Therapeutic sessions involving multiple family members to improve communication, resolve conflicts, and strengthen relationships. Dr. Nolen works collaboratively with families to address challenges and build supportive, effective family dynamics.

🤝

Adolescent Social Skills Groups

Group therapy focused on teaching and practicing social interaction skills in a supportive peer environment. These groups help adolescents build confidence, improve communication, and develop meaningful connections with others.

🏕️

Adventure-Based Group Therapy

Group therapeutic experiences that incorporate outdoor activities and team-based challenges. These sessions foster cooperation, self-awareness, and personal growth through shared adventure and reflection.

Approach to Therapy

Dr. Nolen works with a broad range of life challenges and concerns affecting young people and their families, including:

  • Anxiety, depression, and mood disorders
  • Trauma and post-traumatic stress
  • Behavioral challenges and conduct concerns
  • Autism spectrum and neurodevelopmental disorders
  • Learning difficulties and academic challenges
  • Social skills deficits and peer relationship issues
  • Family conflict and communication difficulties
  • Adjustment to life transitions and stressors

Session Structure

Therapy sessions typically begin on a once-weekly basis, with each session lasting approximately one hour. Dr. Nolen's goal is to reduce session frequency over time as clients gain confidence and experience symptom relief.

This approach reflects Dr. Nolen's philosophy that effective therapy should empower clients to "become their own experts" — building the skills and resilience needed to navigate future challenges independently.

Psychological & Neurodevelopmental Assessments

When available, Dr. Nolen offers comprehensive psychological and neurodevelopmental assessments for children and adolescents. These evaluations are designed to identify strengths, challenges, and the most effective pathways forward — providing clarity for families, educators, and other providers.

Assessment services may include evaluation for:

  • Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
  • Learning disabilities and cognitive concerns
  • Emotional and behavioral functioning
  • Developmental delays and neurodevelopmental conditions
