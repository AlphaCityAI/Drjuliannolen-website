Julian Nolen, Ph.D. • Licensed Psychologist • Helena, Montana

Psychological and educational services for children, adolescents, young adults, families, and organizations seeking support around child and adolescent health and development.

A Practical, Strengths-Based Approach

I provide counseling, assessment, and educational services with an emphasis on evidence-based care, warmth, and real-life application. My work is centered on helping young people, families, and the professionals who support them better understand challenges, build practical skills, and move forward with confidence.

Getting Started

1

Make Initial Contact

Call the office to receive more information about services and specialties, or to request an intake appointment.

2

Discuss Concerns and Fit

Early conversations focus on the presenting concerns and whether the practice is the right fit for your goals.

3

Move Into Intake

Once scheduled, paperwork is completed through the secure portal and the first session focuses on goals, strengths, and next steps.

What to Expect

Phone calls are typically returned within 48 business hours, first sessions are about one hour, and treatment usually begins on a once-weekly basis before tapering as progress is made.
