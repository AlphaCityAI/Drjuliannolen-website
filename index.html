Compassionate, Evidence-Based Care

Dr. Julian Nolen integrates evidence-based techniques with compassion, warmth, and a strengths-based perspective to support children, adolescents, young adults, and families through life's challenges. With over 20 years of professional experience, Dr. Nolen is committed to helping clients develop practical tools, build resilience, and become their own experts.

A Philosophy of Empowerment

Dr. Nolen believes that the most effective therapy helps clients and families acquire the knowledge, skills, and resilience they need to navigate life's obstacles independently. His goal is for each client to eventually "become their own expert" and graduate from services with confidence.

Therapy typically begins with once-weekly, hour-long sessions. As clients gain confidence and experience symptom relief, session frequency is thoughtfully reduced — empowering ongoing growth beyond the therapy room.

Specialized Expertise

Dr. Nolen has supported young people and families across a wide range of settings — including maximum security youth corrections, chronic illness clinics, public high schools, shelters, and private practice — bringing depth and versatility to his clinical work.

Academic Leadership

As Department Chair and Associate Professor at Carroll College, Dr. Nolen also teaches courses in child and adolescent psychology, counseling theory, and experiential therapy — bridging clinical practice with education and research.
